NEOPIXEL
MICROWORLD

This is a semi-structured compilation of example programs to support learners of all ages to explore programming multicolor lights (neopixels) with MakeCode and the BBC micro:bit. Learners can use, modify, and remix beginner, intermediate, and advanced example projects to become familiar with the capabilities of programmable lights.

Blinking Random Colors

LEDs change colors every button press

Beginner

Counter Indicator

LEDs indicate when certain numbers of button presses

Beginner

Dimmer Switch

The buttons make the LEDs bright and dim


Beginner

Jumping Lights

Each button press turns on a random light

Beginner

Color Scale

A random number between 0-10 is shown on the LEDs

Beginner

Color Gradient Graph

Each Button press lights up another light

Beginner

Running Lights

LEDs appear to be moving at different rates

Beginner

Pulsing Lights

LEDs grow brighter and dimmer at different rates

Intermediate

Twinkling Lights

Random LEDs turn on and off with color change on

Advanced

Stress Level Indicator

See the color change as a stress indicator

Beginner

Light Bar Graph

Use neopixels as a bar graph to show light sensed by micro:bit

Intermediate

Rainbow Compass

Check the direction of your micro:bit with a rainbow dot

Intermediate
